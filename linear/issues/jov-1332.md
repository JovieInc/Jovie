## Problem

The public profile currently mixes social icons and mode buttons (tip, contact, listen, etc.) in the same nav area. These serve fundamentally different fan intents:

* **Social icons** → passive discovery (find me on TikTok, Instagram, etc.)
* **Mode buttons** → active engagement (tip me, contact me, listen now)

Conflating them creates visual competition and buries the high-value conversion actions.

## Insight

Fans arriving from a specific platform (e.g. Instagram) won't see that platform's icon anyway (we suppress it). The remaining social icon set is small and finite — scanning for a TikTok icon among 4–5 options is a negligible task. Social icons don't need prime real estate.

Mode buttons — tipping, tour dates, listening — are the actual conversion surface and deserve hierarchy.

## Goal

Find an elegant way to visually separate and prioritize mode buttons over social icons, without cluttering the profile. This is a design exploration — no implementation until a direction is chosen and approved.

## Directions to explore

* **Persistent play button** — always-visible play/listen button; social icons sit to its right; other modes live in a bottom strip or expandable tray
* **Listen Now CTA** — prominent button with Spotify logo + dropdown for other platforms; social icons de-emphasized elsewhere
* **Tab navigation** — full tab bar for modes at the bottom; social icons tucked into profile header area
* **Mode tray** — floating action button that expands into a radial or vertical menu of modes; social icons remain in header
* **Split zones** — modes anchored to bottom, social icons anchored near bio/header; clear spatial separation

## Constraints

* Must not clutter the profile — simplicity is the whole point of Jovie
* Must work across mobile and desktop
* Should feel like a natural evolution of the current design, not a redesign

## Next step

Explore and wireframe the above directions (or propose others). Present options for human review before any code is written. This is a **design decision**, not an engineering task.
