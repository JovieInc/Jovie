## Follow-up

`apps/web/components/organisms/AuthModal.tsx` is the marketing auth modal triggered from the homepage / CTAs. It still renders Clerk `<SignIn>` and `<SignUp>` directly, with its own `ClerkProvider` wrapper and toggle logic, instead of going through the canonical `AuthShell` introduced in [JOV-2064](https://linear.app/jovie/issue/JOV-2064/p0-refactor-signinsignup-page-modal-into-shared-authshell). As a result the marketing modal does NOT benefit from `buildDisabledOAuthProviderElements()` — Apple (or any other provider added by mistake) could still leak through there.

## Why it matters

* Provider guard divergence: [JOV-2062](https://linear.app/jovie/issue/JOV-2062/p0-disable-apple-provider-unless-production-credentials-are-valid) only covers the `(auth)/signin` page, `(auth)/signup` page, and the intercepted `@auth/(.)signup` modal. The marketing AuthModal is a third surface where Apple could re-appear.
* Code-model divergence: marketing modal uses its own ClerkProvider, appearance object, and mode-toggle UX. Diverges from the AuthShell contract.

## Classification

Candidate

## Acceptance criteria or triage question

Pickup agent must first judge whether to migrate marketing AuthModal to AuthShell (consistent provider gating, single content model) or leave it as a deliberate "marketing-only Clerk modal" surface and instead add the provider guard inline. If migrating, the AuthShell needs to support the marketing modal's compact dark appearance and inline mode toggle.
