## Why it matters

CEO/Eng/DX subagent consensus: "abandoned overhead in 60 days" is the high-confidence regret scenario for executing Phase 2-3 at current stage. The Phase 0 instrumentation artifact is the one-time-read value. The perpetual machinery (ratchet, mock-budget lint, monthly reviews) is process larping unless there's traction-driven volume to justify it. At $50K MRR the math flips: regressions cost real revenue, agent throughput needs hardening, and Tim has bandwidth to maintain the system.

## Classification

Candidate (with trigger condition: $50K MRR OR product-market signal that justifies investment in long-tail regression prevention)

Pickup agent must first judge whether to implement, close, or split this.

## Trigger / decision question

When this issue comes up for triage:

1. Has Jovie reached $50K MRR or product-market signal? If no, snooze.
2. Has agent-shipping volume (PRs/week) grown to the point where regressions are costing meaningful time? If no, snooze.
3. Has the heatmap actually been used (reads, PR comments) over the past 30 days? If no, the system has been validated as low-value at this stage — close this issue and delete the heatmap infrastructure.
4. If yes to 1+2 AND yes to 3: pick the lowest-cost / highest-leverage sub-bullet first (likely: Stryker `break: 60` enforcement and the mock-budget eslint rule — both are surgical adjustments to existing infra).
