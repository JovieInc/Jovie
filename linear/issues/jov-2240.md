## Follow-up

v1 uses cron polling for Gmail history sync. At beta scale (5–10 users) this is fine. When poll volume becomes the limiting factor for either freshness ("new booking sat in inbox for 12 minutes before Jovie saw it") or cost (Gmail API quota), wire Pub/Sub:

* Google Cloud project Pub/Sub topic
* Gmail `watch()` per connector_account
* Verification token, webhook signature check, durable dedupe via `webhook_deliveries` (already exists from C-PR-1)
* Renewal cron (Gmail watch expires every 7 days)

## Why it matters

Pub/Sub is significant ops yak (project setup, IAM, service accounts, verification). Worth it only when polling becomes a problem. Don't pre-build.

## Classification

Candidate

Pickup agent must first judge whether to implement, close, or split this. Triggering condition: any of (a) >50 active beta users, (b) median poll-to-suggestion latency >10 min, (c) Gmail quota usage >50% of limit.

## Dependency

Depends on C-PR-2 (Gmail polling baseline) and H-PR-2 (webhook_deliveries usage pattern).
