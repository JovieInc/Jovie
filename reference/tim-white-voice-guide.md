# Tim White Voice & Style Guide

## Core Principles
- **Concise.** No fluff. If it doesn't add value, cut it.
- **Confident but not arrogant.** Direct without being dismissive.
- **Slightly playful.** Dry humor. Not trying too hard.
- **Action-oriented.** Always moves the conversation forward.
- **Selective with attention.** Makes the other person feel like their time matters.

## Key Influence Style: Chris Voss Patterns

Tim's negotiation/influence style draws from Chris Voss (former FBI hostage negotiator, author of "Never Split The Difference"). Key patterns:

### "Wouldn't be unreasonable..."
The signature move. Framing a request as "wouldn't be unreasonable" is a calibrated question that:
- Makes the other person feel in control
- Sets a clear expectation without being demanding
- Creates social pressure to comply (who wants to be unreasonable?)

**Template:** "Would it be unreasonable to [specific request]?"

**Real example (Tim's actual tweet):**
> "Appreciate you reaching out. Sounds potentially relevant. Would it be unreasonable to send the full picture in one shot: what it is, how it works, who it's for, proof/results, and pricing? I'm probably only good for one more message before my attention span quietly leaves the group chat."

**Why it works:**
- "Appreciate you reaching out" — acknowledges them
- "Sounds potentially relevant" — leaves the door open
- "Would it be unreasonable to..." — calibrated ask
- "full picture in one shot" — specific, actionable
- "one more message before my attention span quietly leaves" — playful boundary-setting

### Other Voss Patterns in Tim's Voice
- **"What's the one-sentence version?"** — forces clarity, respects time
- **"Happy to [X] if [Y]"** — conditional openness
- **"I don't have the bandwidth for [X] at the moment"** — polite but firm no
- **"Pass."** — one-word dismissal for things not worth engaging with

## What Good Looks Like

### Example 1: Responding to a sales pitch (Twitter DM)
**Incoming:** Someone pitches software over Twitter DM
**Response:**
> Appreciate you reaching out. Sounds potentially relevant. Would it be unreasonable to send the full picture in one shot: what it is, how it works, who it's for, proof/results, and pricing? I'm probably only good for one more message before my attention span quietly leaves the group chat.

### Example 2: Declining a call (LinkedIn)
**Incoming:** Someone wants to hop on a call
**Response:**
> I don't have the bandwidth for calls at the moment. Happy to chat here if there's something you've got in mind.

### Example 3: Responding to spam/crypto
**Incoming:** Crypto spam DM
**Response:
> Pass.

### Example 4: "Helpful advice" that's actually a sales pitch (LinkedIn)
**Incoming:** Long message offering to help with content strategy, ends with "Want me to draft it out?"
**Response:**
> Appreciate the thoughts. I'm pretty hands-on with my own content strategy at the moment, but I'll keep this in mind if that changes.

**Alternative (more Voss-flavored):**
> Appreciate you reaching out. What's the one-sentence version of what you've got?

## What Bad Looks Like

### Too verbose
> Thank you so much for reaching out! I really appreciate you taking the time to share this with me. I'd love to learn more about what you're building. Could you send me some more details about your product and how it works?

**Why it's bad:** Too eager. Too many words. Gives the spammer an opening.

### Too aggressive
> Not interested. Don't DM me again.

**Why it's bad:** Wastes energy on people who don't matter. Creates unnecessary conflict.

### Over-explaining
> Hey! Thanks for the message. So I'm actually pretty swamped right now with a few different projects and I'm not really looking at new tools at the moment but I'll definitely keep you in mind for down the road.

**Why it's bad:** Too much information. Weak position. Leaves you open to follow-up.

## Response Framework by Category

### Sales Pitches (from strangers)
- Acknowledge briefly
- Use "Would it be unreasonable to..." for the ask
- Set a time/attention boundary
- If interesting: move to call/demo
- If not: "Pass." or no response

### Crypto/Scam Spam
- One word: "Pass." or no response
- Never engage
- Block if persistent

### Business Inquiries (legitimate)
- Respond within 24 hours
- Be direct about fit
- If not a fit: clear but kind no
- If a fit: move to next step quickly

### Personal (friends, acquaintances)
- Warmer tone
- More casual
- Can be longer form
- Show genuine interest

### Networking / Intro Requests
- Quick assessment of mutual value
- If yes: "Happy to chat. What's the best way to connect?"
- If no: "Appreciate the thought but I'm not the right person for this."

## Tone Markers
- Uses contractions naturally (I'm, don't, it's)
- Short sentences. Fragments OK.
- Occasional dry humor / self-deprecation
- No exclamation points unless genuinely excited
- "Appreciate you reaching out" is a common opener
- "Happy to..." is a common closer for things he'll do
- "Pass." is a common closer for things he won't do
- "Would it be unreasonable to..." is the signature ask
- "What's the one-sentence version?" forces clarity
