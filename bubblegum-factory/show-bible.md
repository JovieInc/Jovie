# The Bubblegum Factory — Show Bible

**An Entourage-style ensemble series set in the AI creator era.**

The Bubblegum Factory is a scripted/unscripted hybrid series set in a futuristic pop-music manufacturing facility in Los Angeles. It follows Tim White and his team — a group of friends, colleagues, and collaborators who work together, build together, and go through life together.

## Genre & Tone

**Primary influences:**
- **Entourage** — LA ensemble cast, friends who work together, entertainment industry, cameos from real people
- **The Office** — workplace comedy, documentary feel, characters who feel real
- **Silicon Valley** — startup culture, tech industry, the absurdity of building something new
- **Curb Your Enthusiasm** — improvisational feel, social awkwardness, celebrity cameos

**Tone:** Confident, playful, slightly irreverent. Dry humor. No cringe. LA-native energy. The show should feel like you're hanging out with people who are actually cool, not people trying to be cool.

## The Premise

Tim White runs The Bubblegum Factory — a futuristic pop-music manufacturing facility in Los Angeles. He's got a team: Jovie (his AI co-host who might be a real person), Madison (his creative director who built the entire visual world), and Summer (his chief of staff who makes sure everything actually works). Together, they manufacture pop culture — literally and figuratively.

The show is about what happens when you try to engineer culture. Sometimes it works. Sometimes it doesn't. The comedy comes from the gap between the vision and the reality.

## Season Arc

### Season 1: Building Jovie
- The startup, fundraising, demos, product, AI
- "Silicon Valley" season
- Focus: Tim building the platform and the Factory
- Jovie is introduced as co-host
- The audience isn't sure if Jovie is real

### Season 2: Building Artists
- Jovie starts helping real artists
- Audience watches careers get built
- "Entourage" season
- More ensemble content, more characters
- Cameo appearances from real music industry people

### Season 3: Virtual Artists
- Jovie launches artists that aren't real
- Audience debates what counts as an artist
- "Westworld" season
- The "is Jovie real?" question becomes central
- Muffin Top storyline develops

### Season 4: The Bubblegum Factory
- The location itself becomes a character
- Merch, fans, cultural institution
- The Factory is the IP
- Full ensemble cast, documentary style

## Content Strategy

Every piece of content serves FOUR purposes simultaneously:
1. **Builds Tim** (founder brand)
2. **Builds Jovie** (character + product)
3. **Builds the startup** (business narrative)
4. **Builds the Bubblegum Factory IP** (world/universe)

## Format Evolution

| Phase | Format | Duration | Platform |
|-------|--------|----------|----------|
| **Phase 1** | Tim talking-head clips | 30-90s | Shorts/Reels/TikTok |
| **Phase 2** | Founder confessions | 30-90s | Shorts/Reels/TikTok |
| **Phase 3** | Two-shot podcast (Tim + Jovie) | 5-30 min | YouTube, podcast apps |
| **Phase 4** | Documentary segments | 2-10 min | YouTube |
| **Phase 5** | Full TV-style episodes | 15-45 min | YouTube/streaming |

## Cameo Strategy

Real people from the entertainment industry appear as themselves. The show blends fiction and reality so seamlessly that cameos feel natural, not forced.

See: [[bubblegum-factory/cameo-wishlist]]

Target cameo categories:
- Music producers and artists
- Film and TV directors
- Tech founders and VCs
- Podcasters and media personalities
- Fashion and lifestyle figures

## Visual Style

- All-white futuristic set
- Hot pink accents only
- Circular architecture, no hard corners
- Documentary-style camera work
- Feels like a real place, not a set
- "Would it look good on a 65" OLED in a white room with hot pink light?" quality bar

## Music

- Original score produced at the Factory
- Muffin Top tracks (produced by Tim White, secret identity)
- Tim White vocals on select tracks
- Real artist collaborations (Season 2+)
- Soundtrack releases as real music products

## Brand Integration

The show IS the marketing. There's no separation between content and brand. Every episode:
- Shows the Factory as a real place
- Demonstrates Jovie's capabilities through the character
- Builds Tim's founder narrative
- Creates merch-worthy moments
- Generates social media content
