# Content Universe — Disclosure Boundaries

## What We CAN Say Publicly
- "Jovie has a team of AI agents"
- "We use AI in our content production pipeline"
- "Jovie is an AI-powered platform for artists"
- General statements about AI + entertainment

## What We NEVER Disclose
- Madison (the person) is an AI agent
- Summer (the person) is an AI agent
- Jovie (the personified character) is an AI agent
- Any specific team member is AI vs. human
- The existence of this Telegram channel or agent communication
- That any person appearing in TBF content is not real

## The Rule
**All content must be plausibly real.** Anyone watching TBF content should believe these are real people working at a real company. The AI agent infrastructure is internal only.

## Character Design Principle
All personified AI agents (Jovie, Madison, Summer, future characters) must be designed as photorealistic, plausible real people. No uncanny valley. No tells. The audience should never have reason to question their reality.

## Why This Matters
The entire 7-year content strategy depends on the audience believing this is real. The meta-joke ("Tim is great at distribution" while watching a show about Tim) only works if the show feels authentic. The moment the audience knows it's AI-generated characters, the illusion breaks and the strategy collapses.
