# BUBBLEGUM FACTORY — CANON AI PROMPTS

All prompts for generating images and video of The Bubblegum Factory universe.
Copy-paste ready. Paste master style block at top of every prompt.

## 0. MASTER STYLE BLOCK

**ALWAYS paste this at the top of every generation prompt.**

```
The Bubblegum Factory is a futuristic pop-music manufacturing studio in Los Angeles. The visual language is all-white, glossy, circular, minimalist, high-end, and slightly absurd. It feels like a luxury recording studio, a pop laboratory, a spaceship interior, and an architectural magazine feature blended together. No hard corners. Rounded walls, circular rooms, curved furniture, seamless white acoustic panels, soft backlit LED seams, translucent plexiglass details, brushed aluminum floor grates, and one accent color only: vivid bubblegum hot pink. The space should feel expensive, iconic, clean, mysterious, and culturally important.

The studio is not cyberpunk, not dark, not cluttered, not industrial warehouse, not nightclub, not hospital, not sterile medical lab, not generic spaceship, not corporate office. It should feel like a place where pop culture is manufactured.
```

## 1. PODCAST ROOM — MASTER WIDE

**Canonical set design. First image to lock.**

```
Create a photorealistic cinematic podcast room inside The Bubblegum Factory, a futuristic all-white pop-music manufacturing studio in Los Angeles.

The room is perfectly circular, with curved all-white acoustic wall panels arranged around the full perimeter. The walls have soft horizontal LED seams that wrap around the room. The ceiling has a large circular recessed light ring and padded white acoustic ceiling panels. The space feels like a luxury recording studio, a futuristic pop laboratory, and an architectural magazine interior.

At the center is a large monolithic circular white podcast table, glossy and sculptural, with a thick cylindrical pedestal base that hides all cables and hardware. The table is large enough for two people, four people, or a full roundtable conversation, while still allowing clean camera angles from any direction. Professional black SM7B-style microphones sit on minimal white boom arms around the table. Only a few microphones should be visible in the shot.

The seating is white, minimal, and partially hidden behind the table so feet and chair bases are not visible in normal camera angles. The room should feel designed for cinematic podcast shots where the camera can move anywhere around the circular table.

On the rear wall is a wall-mounted TV screen centered above the table sight-line. The TV is surrounded only by soft white backlighting (not pink backlighting). The TV displays the Jovie logo as a placeholder show logo. The screen is a clean placeholder — future generations can swap any logo or content onto it.

Etch a subtle Jovie-logo-inspired line into the surface of the table: a thin recessed circular groove with a small curved comma-like break near the front-right area, matching the Jovie icon geometry. The groove should look machined into the glossy white surface, filled with soft white light or slightly darker shadow, not physically cut through the table. It should feel like a premium product-design detail, not a gap or missing piece.

On the rear wall is a large hot pink Bubblegum Factory mark. The logo should look stamped, painted, or stenciled directly onto the wall, like a bold industrial flight-case stamp or factory inspection mark. Thick block letters, slightly imperfect stamped edges, vivid bubblegum hot pink. It should feel manufactured, branded, and iconic. Do not make it look like thin neon tubing. It may glow subtly from spill light, but the main effect is stamped hot pink ink or paint.

Materials: matte white acoustic fabric, glossy white polymer table, soft hidden LEDs, translucent white acrylic, subtle brushed aluminum hardware, black professional microphones. The only accent color is vivid bubblegum hot pink.

Camera: wide master shot from slightly above table height, symmetrical composition, 24mm lens, cinematic architectural realism, soft white studio lighting, high dynamic range, no people, no readable small text, no clutter, no visible cables, no dark cyberpunk lighting, no corporate office feel.
```

## USAGE NOTES

1. **Always prepend Prompt 0 (Master Style Block)** to every generation.
2. **Always append Prompt 17 (Text/Logo Instruction)** to every generation.
3. **Use Prompt 16 (Style Guardrail)** when results drift toward cyberpunk/dark/neon.
4. **Room locks matter**: Always cross-reference the locked podcast room design when generating other rooms. The world must feel unified.
5. **Reference images**: Supply actual photos of Tim when generating any prompt with a person. AI without reference will drift.
6. **Post-production text**: Every piece of readable text in any final output should be layered in post, not baked into the generation.
