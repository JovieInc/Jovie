# BUBBLEGUM FACTORY — FACILITY ARCHITECTURE

Technical reference for all rooms, camera systems, and production design specs.

## Design Philosophy

The Bubblegum Factory should feel like a **real place** that could exist in Los Angeles. Every room must feel:
- Architecturally sound (would pass an architect's review)
- Functionally real (every design choice has a production reason)
- Iconic (immediately recognizable as "the Factory")
- Repeatable (can be used for hundreds of episodes)

#### Camera System Detail
The defining feature of the podcast room is the **sliding acoustic panel camera system**:
- Wall panels arranged in two horizontal rows (top and bottom)
- Panels slide apart in the middle to reveal a glass camera port
- Camera shoots through glass into the room
- When panels slide closed, wall is seamless — no visible camera positions
- This allows **360° camera angles** from any wall position
- Panel movement is powered, quiet, and can be controlled remotely

#### Standard Camera Positions

| Position | Name | Description |
|----------|------|-------------|
| A | Tim Primary | Table-height, chest-up, Tim framed right-of-center |
| B | Reverse / Jovie | Empty guest position or Jovie angle |
| C | Two-Shot | Slightly wide, showing both host positions |
| D | Overhead | Looking down at table from ceiling height |
| E | Low Angle | Table edge dominant, looking up at speaker |
| F | Environment | Wide establishing shot, full room |

#### Blast Doors
- Behind the main camera position (where the audience "is")
- All-white, rounded, sci-fi-inspired but luxurious
- Not visible in standard A/B/C camera positions
- Visible in wide establishing shots from specific angles

## Lighting Design

| Scenario | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Standard | Soft white studio, even, flattering, shadow-free |
| Dramatic | Selective LED seams, deeper shadows, moody |
| Accent | Hot pink emissive from logo only |
| Lobby Hero | Soft daylight + hidden LED, editorial feel |

No dark lighting. No neon. No harsh shadows. Everything should feel expensive and intentional.
