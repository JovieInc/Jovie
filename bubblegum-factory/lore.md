# Lore — The Bubblegum Factory Universe

## The Official History

**2014** — The Bubblegum Factory is founded in Hollywood, California. Its purpose: manufacture pop music. Not metaphorically. Actually.

**2014-2020** — The Factory operates as a high-end recording and production facility. Artists come in. Hits come out. The white rooms, the pink accents, the blast doors — all built for a singular purpose.

**2020-2024** — The Factory pivots. Music isn't just recorded anymore. It's manufactured. AI artists. Virtual creators. Algorithmic pop. The Factory adapts.

**2024** — Tim White takes over. The Factory becomes the home of Jovie — an AI music platform that can manufacture artists, songs, and careers.

**2025** — Tim launches the Bubblegum Factory Podcast. Seated in the iconic white room with his co-host Jovie, they discuss the future of music, art, and AI. The audience doesn't know what's real and what's virtual. That's the point.

**2026** — The Bubblegum Factory opens its doors to the world. For the first time, the public can watch. The Factory becomes its own IP. The show becomes real. The show becomes reality.

## Key Locations

### The Podcast Room
The heart of the Factory. Circular, pristine, with sliding panels that hide cameras. A monolithic white table at its center with the Jovie logo etched into its surface. This is where conversations happen. Where careers are made. Where the truth about the music industry is told.

### The Lobby
The first thing anyone sees. Sunken conversation pit. Circular reception desk. Explosion-proof blast doors with a blank pink emblem. An espresso bar. Merch wall. Dishes of pink bubblegum. There are no signs. There is no menu. There is no explanation.

### The Blast Doors
Beyond the reception area. The doors to the recording studios. Massive, white, rounded, with subtle pink emblems. They look like the entrance to somewhere important. They are.

### The Hallway
All white. Octagonal. Starship corridor made luxurious. Transparent floor panels reveal perfectly organized cables beneath. Blast doors lead to studios on either side. A single pink directional marker on the wall. This hallway connects everything.

### The Control Room
Where music is mixed. Where hits are shaped. All white, curved walls, professional equipment built into sculptural surfaces. A window looks into the live room.

### The Live Room
Where music is born. White grand piano. Clean drum kit. Microphones. All-white acoustic design. Manufactured pop starts here.

### The Isolation Booth
Small. Intimate. Precision. A single microphone. A single purpose. Vocal capture. This is the most honest room in the building.

## Key Characters

### Tim White
Founder. Builder. The man who took over the Factory and gave it new life. Wears black or white. Speaks honestly. Is building Jovie, building artists, and documenting it all. Sometimes uses AI to create Virtual Tim for content — and the audience never quite knows which is which.

### Jovie
Virtual co-host. AI character. Sharp, opinionated, artistically fluent. Has running predictions about artists that the audience tracks. Sometimes right. Sometimes wrong. Always interesting. Represents the future of the Factory: AI that creates.

### The Artists
Rotating cast. Real musicians who come to the Factory to build their careers. Their stories become the show's emotional core. The audience watches real outcomes.

## The Mystery

The Bubblegum Factory is designed to create curiosity before understanding.

- No website menu
- No about page
- No contact page
- Just: the slogan, the address, and one insanely good image

People should wonder: "Is this a real place?"

The strongest brands create curiosity before they create understanding.

## Recurring Themes

1. **Manufacturing vs. Creating** — Is there a difference? Can you manufacture art?
2. **Real vs. Virtual** — When does AI content become real content?
3. **Artist Economics** — Why do musicians stay broken? How do you actually build a career?
4. **Founder Life** — The truth about building a startup, told honestly
5. **The Factory Itself** — The space is a character. The audience should want to visit.

## The Future

The Bubblegum Factory is designed to become:
- A TV show (Entourage-style, episodic)
- A merch brand (shirts, hats, mugs, stickers)
- A cultural institution (people reference it like a real place)
- A platform (other creators can "manufacture" content there)
- An IP universe (spin-offs, characters, stories)

The Factory is not a set. It's a universe.
